NELFUND STUDENT LOAN - REPAYMENT TERMS AND COVERED INSTITUTIONS
================================================================

PART 1: REPAYMENT TERMS AND CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------

1. REPAYMENT COMMENCEMENT
-------------------------

When Repayment Starts:
- Repayment begins TWO (2) years after completion of National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) program
- For graduates who do not participate in NYSC, repayment begins two years after graduation
- This grace period allows graduates to secure employment and settle into their careers

Grace Period Details:
- No repayment required during the 2-year grace period
- Interest does not accrue (loans are interest-free)
- Administrative fees may apply during this period
- Graduates can make voluntary payments during grace period

2. REPAYMENT AMOUNT CALCULATION
-------------------------------

Repayment Percentage:
- 10% of monthly income for employed graduates
- This percentage is fixed and applies to all borrowers
- Calculated from gross monthly income before deductions

Minimum Repayment:
- A minimum repayment amount is set by NELFUND
- Even if 10% of income is below minimum, minimum amount applies
- Minimum amount ensures reasonable repayment timeline

Maximum Repayment:
- No maximum limit on monthly repayment
- Borrowers can pay more than 10% if they wish
- Early repayment is encouraged and allowed

3. REPAYMENT METHODS
--------------------

Method 1: Direct Salary Deduction
- For employed graduates
- Employer deducts 10% from salary
- Automatically remitted to NELFUND
- Most convenient method
- Requires employer cooperation

Method 2: Bank Transfer via Portal
- Log in to NELFUND portal
- Navigate to "Repayment" section
- Enter amount to pay
- Complete bank transfer
- Upload payment receipt

Method 3: Mobile Banking
- Use bank mobile app
- Transfer to NELFUND account
- Include reference number
- Save transaction receipt

Method 4: Automatic Bank Debit
- Set up standing order
- Bank automatically debits account monthly
- Requires bank account details
- Most reliable for consistent payments

4. REPAYMENT SCHEDULE
---------------------

Monthly Repayments:
- Repayments are made monthly
- Due date is typically first week of each month
- Late payments may incur penalties
- Reminders sent via email and SMS

Repayment Duration:
- Depends on loan amount and income
- Higher income = faster repayment
- Lower income = longer repayment period
- Example: N500,000 loan at N20,000/month = 25 months

5. EARLY REPAYMENT
------------------

Benefits of Early Repayment:
- Reduces total repayment period
- No penalties for early repayment
- Can make partial or full early payments
- Improves credit history

How to Make Early Repayment:
- Log in to NELFUND portal
- Go to "Early Repayment" section
- Enter amount to pay
- Complete payment
- Updated balance reflected immediately

6. DEFERMENT AND FORBEARANCE
-----------------------------

Deferment Eligibility:
- Unemployed graduates
- Further education (masters, PhD)
- National service (NYSC, if not completed)
- Medical conditions
- Economic hardship

How to Apply for Deferment:
- Submit deferment request through portal
- Provide supporting documents
- Explain reason for deferment
- Approval typically takes 5-10 working days

Forbearance:
- Temporary reduction or pause in payments
- Available for financial hardship
- Must demonstrate inability to pay
- Requires regular review and renewal

7. DEFAULT AND CONSEQUENCES
---------------------------

What Constitutes Default:
- Missing three consecutive monthly payments
- Failure to respond to payment reminders
- Providing false information to avoid payment
- Refusing to cooperate with repayment process

Consequences of Default:
- Blacklisting from future loans
- Legal action may be taken
- Credit score affected
- May affect guarantor
- Possible asset seizure (in extreme cases)
- Inability to access other government loans

Avoiding Default:
- Set up automatic payments
- Contact NELFUND if facing difficulties
- Apply for deferment if eligible
- Make partial payments if full payment not possible
- Keep communication open with NELFUND

8. LOAN FORGIVENESS
--------------------

Circumstances for Loan Forgiveness:
- Death of borrower (loan written off)
- Permanent disability preventing employment
- Special circumstances as determined by NELFUND board
- Public service in certain sectors (may have special provisions)

PART 2: COVERED INSTITUTIONS
-----------------------------

1. FEDERAL UNIVERSITIES
-----------------------

All federal universities in Nigeria are covered, including:

North Central:
- University of Abuja, Abuja
- Federal University of Technology, Minna
- Federal University, Lokoja
- And all other federal universities in the region

North East:
- University of Maiduguri
- Federal University, Wukari
- Federal University, Gashua
- And all other federal universities in the region

North West:
- Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria
- Bayero University, Kano
- Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto
- And all other federal universities in the region

South East:
- University of Nigeria, Nsukka
- Nnamdi Azikiwe University, Awka
- Federal University of Technology, Owerri
- And all other federal universities in the region

South South:
- University of Benin
- University of Calabar
- Federal University of Petroleum Resources, Effurun
- And all other federal universities in the region

South West:
- University of Lagos
- Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife
- University of Ibadan
- And all other federal universities in the region

2. STATE UNIVERSITIES
---------------------

All state-owned universities are covered, including:
- State universities across all 36 states
- Must be accredited by National Universities Commission (NUC)
- Must be recognized by Federal Ministry of Education
- Includes specialized state universities (agriculture, technology, etc.)

3. FEDERAL POLYTECHNICS
-----------------------

All federal polytechnics are covered, including:
- Yaba College of Technology, Lagos
- Federal Polytechnic, Nekede
- Federal Polytechnic, Ilaro
- Federal Polytechnic, Bauchi
- And all other federal polytechnics nationwide

4. STATE POLYTECHNICS
---------------------

All state-owned polytechnics are covered:
- Must be accredited by National Board for Technical Education (NBTE)
- Must be recognized by relevant authorities
- Includes monotechnics and technical colleges

5. FEDERAL COLLEGES OF EDUCATION
--------------------------------

All federal colleges of education are covered:
- Federal College of Education (Technical), Akoka
- Federal College of Education, Zaria
- And all other federal colleges of education

6. STATE COLLEGES OF EDUCATION
-------------------------------

All state-owned colleges of education are covered:
- Must be accredited by National Commission for Colleges of Education (NCCE)
- Must be recognized by relevant authorities

7. TECHNICAL AND VOCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS
-----------------------------------------

Public technical and vocational institutions are covered:
- Must be accredited by relevant regulatory bodies
- Must offer recognized qualifications
- Includes technical colleges and vocational training centers

8. INSTITUTIONS NOT COVERED
---------------------------

The following are NOT eligible:
- Private universities
- Private polytechnics
- Private colleges of education
- Private technical colleges
- International institutions
- Unaccredited institutions
- Distance learning programs from private institutions

9. VERIFICATION OF INSTITUTION ELIGIBILITY
-----------------------------------------

How to Verify Your Institution:
1. Check NELFUND portal for list of covered institutions
2. Verify with your institution's administration
3. Check accreditation status with relevant regulatory body:
   - Universities: National Universities Commission (NUC)
   - Polytechnics: National Board for Technical Education (NBTE)
   - Colleges of Education: National Commission for Colleges of Education (NCCE)

10. UPDATED INSTITUTION LIST
-----------------------------

The list of covered institutions is updated regularly:
- New institutions may be added
- Institutions may be removed if accreditation is lost
- Check NELFUND portal for most current list
- Contact NELFUND if your institution is not listed but should be eligible

IMPORTANT NOTES
---------------

- Only PUBLIC institutions are covered
- Institution must be properly accredited
- Student must be enrolled in recognized program
- Institution must be located in Nigeria
- Distance learning from public institutions may be considered on case-by-case basis
- Always verify current eligibility status before applying
