NELFUND STUDENT LOAN - DETAILED ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
=====================================================

WHO IS ELIGIBLE FOR NELFUND STUDENT LOAN?
------------------------------------------

1. CITIZENSHIP REQUIREMENT
   - Must be a Nigerian citizen
   - Must have valid proof of citizenship (National ID, International Passport, or Birth Certificate)
   - Dual citizens are eligible if they hold Nigerian citizenship

2. INSTITUTION REQUIREMENTS
   - Must be enrolled in a PUBLIC tertiary institution in Nigeria
   - Institution must be accredited by relevant regulatory bodies:
     * Universities: Accredited by National Universities Commission (NUC)
     * Polytechnics: Accredited by National Board for Technical Education (NBTE)
     * Colleges of Education: Accredited by National Commission for Colleges of Education (NCCE)
   - Private institutions are NOT eligible
   - Institution must be recognized by the Federal Ministry of Education

3. ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS
   - Must have valid JAMB (Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board) admission letter
   - Must have institution admission letter
   - Must be admitted through proper admission process
   - Admission must be for a recognized course/program

4. IDENTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
   - Must have valid National Identification Number (NIN)
   - Must have valid Bank Verification Number (BVN)
   - Both NIN and BVN must be linked and verified
   - Information on NIN and BVN must match applicant's details

5. INCOME REQUIREMENTS (For Maintenance Loan)
   - Family annual income must be below N500,000 to qualify for maintenance allowance
   - Family income includes combined income of parents/guardians
   - Must provide verifiable proof of income (tax returns, pay slips, affidavit)
   - Students from families earning above N500,000 can still get tuition loan but not maintenance allowance

6. ACADEMIC REQUIREMENTS
   - Must maintain satisfactory academic progress (minimum CGPA as required by institution)
   - Must not be on academic probation
   - Must not have been suspended or expelled
   - For continuing students: Must have passed at least 70% of registered courses
   - Must complete course within approved duration (no excessive extension)

7. GUARANTOR REQUIREMENTS
   - Must provide a guarantor (parent, guardian, or close relative)
   - Guarantor must be a Nigerian citizen
   - Guarantor must have valid NIN and BVN
   - Guarantor must be employed or have verifiable source of income
   - Guarantor must not be a loan defaulter
   - Guarantor must be willing to assume responsibility if borrower defaults

8. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
   - Must provide all required documents (see Required Documents list)
   - All documents must be authentic and verifiable
   - Documents must be clear and legible
   - Must not have submitted false information in previous applications

9. PREVIOUS LOAN STATUS
   - Must not have defaulted on any previous NELFUND loan
   - Must not have defaulted on any other government loan
   - Must not be blacklisted by any financial institution
   - Previous loan must be fully repaid or in good standing

10. AGE REQUIREMENTS
    - Must be at least 18 years old (or have parental consent if younger)
    - No maximum age limit for eligible students
    - Must be of sound mind and capable of entering into legal agreement

11. COURSE/PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS
    - Course must be accredited by relevant professional bodies
    - Course must lead to recognized qualification (degree, diploma, certificate)
    - Must be a full-time program (part-time programs may have different requirements)
    - Course duration must be within approved limits

12. INSTITUTION LOCATION
    - Institution must be located in Nigeria
    - Both federal and state institutions are eligible
    - Distance learning programs from public institutions may be considered

WHO IS NOT ELIGIBLE?
--------------------

1. Students in private institutions
2. Students without valid NIN or BVN
3. Students whose family income exceeds N500,000 (for maintenance loan only)
4. Students on academic probation or suspension
5. Students who have defaulted on previous loans
6. Students who provided false information
7. Non-Nigerian citizens
8. Students in unaccredited institutions
9. Students without valid admission letters
10. Students who cannot provide a qualified guarantor

SPECIAL CASES
-------------

1. ORPHANS AND VULNERABLE CHILDREN
   - Special consideration may be given
   - May require additional documentation from social welfare
   - Guarantor requirements may be adjusted

2. STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES
   - Fully eligible if they meet other criteria
   - Additional support may be available
   - Special documentation may be required

3. STUDENTS FROM DISPLACED COMMUNITIES
   - Eligible if they meet standard criteria
   - May require additional verification
   - Special consideration for documentation challenges

4. TRANSFER STUDENTS
   - Eligible if transferring to another eligible public institution
   - Must provide transfer documentation
   - Previous academic records must be verified

5. PART-TIME STUDENTS
   - Eligibility may vary
   - Must check specific requirements for part-time programs
   - May have different loan terms

VERIFICATION PROCESS
--------------------

NELFUND verifies eligibility through:
1. Cross-checking with JAMB database
2. Verifying with institution records
3. Checking NIN and BVN databases
4. Verifying guarantor information
5. Income verification through tax records
6. Academic record verification
7. Previous loan history check

APPLICATION TIPS FOR ELIGIBILITY
---------------------------------

1. Ensure all documents are authentic and up-to-date
2. Verify your NIN and BVN are properly linked
3. Maintain good academic standing
4. Choose a qualified guarantor
5. Provide accurate income information
6. Apply early to allow time for verification
7. Respond promptly to verification requests
8. Keep copies of all submitted documents
