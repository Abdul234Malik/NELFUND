NIGERIAN EDUCATION LOAN FUND (NELFUND) - COMPREHENSIVE INFORMATION
====================================================================

1. STUDENT LOAN (ACCESS TO HIGHER EDUCATION) ACT 2023
-----------------------------------------------------

The Student Loan (Access to Higher Education) Act 2023 was signed into law to provide interest-free loans to Nigerian students pursuing higher education in public tertiary institutions.

Key Provisions:
- Interest-free loans for eligible students
- Covers tuition fees and maintenance allowances
- Repayment begins two years after completion of NYSC or graduation
- Managed by the Nigerian Education Loan Fund (NELFUND)

2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
------------------------

To be eligible for NELFUND student loan, applicants must meet the following criteria:

a) Institution Requirements:
- Must be enrolled in a public tertiary institution in Nigeria
- Institution must be accredited by relevant regulatory bodies
- Includes: Federal and State Universities, Polytechnics, Colleges of Education, and Technical Colleges

b) Student Requirements:
- Must be a Nigerian citizen
- Must have been admitted to an eligible institution
- Must have a valid JAMB admission letter
- Must have a valid Bank Verification Number (BVN)
- Must have a valid National Identification Number (NIN)
- Family income must be below N500,000 per annum (for maintenance loan component)
- Must provide guarantor (parent, guardian, or close relative)
- Must not have defaulted on any previous loan

c) Academic Requirements:
- Must maintain satisfactory academic progress
- Must not be on academic probation
- Must complete the course of study within the approved duration

3. APPLICATION PROCEDURES
--------------------------

Step 1: Registration
- Visit the official NELFUND portal at www.nelf.gov.ng
- Create an account using your email address
- Verify your email address through the confirmation link sent

Step 2: Complete Application Form
- Log in to your account
- Fill out the online application form with accurate information
- Upload required documents (see Required Documents section)

Step 3: Document Verification
- NELFUND will verify all submitted documents
- This process may take 5-10 working days
- You will receive email notifications about verification status

Step 4: Approval and Disbursement
- Upon approval, loan amount will be disbursed directly to your institution
- Maintenance allowance (if applicable) will be paid to your bank account
- You will receive confirmation via email and SMS

Step 5: Acceptance
- Sign the loan agreement document
- Acknowledge understanding of repayment terms and conditions

4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
---------------------

Applicants must provide the following documents:

1. Valid JAMB Admission Letter
2. Institution Admission Letter
3. Valid National Identification Number (NIN) slip
4. Bank Verification Number (BVN) certificate
5. Recent passport photograph
6. Academic transcripts (for continuing students)
7. Guarantor's information and documents:
   - Guarantor's NIN
   - Guarantor's BVN
   - Guarantor's employment letter or business registration
   - Guarantor's recent bank statement
8. Family income statement or affidavit
9. Institution's fee structure/breakdown
10. Valid email address and phone number

5. LOAN AMOUNTS AND COVERAGE
------------------------------

a) Tuition Fees:
- Full coverage of tuition fees as approved by the institution
- Paid directly to the institution
- Covers all academic sessions until graduation

b) Maintenance Allowance:
- Up to N50,000 per session for eligible students
- Paid directly to student's bank account
- Eligibility based on family income (below N500,000 per annum)
- Covers accommodation, feeding, and other living expenses

6. REPAYMENT TERMS
-------------------

a) Repayment Commencement:
- Repayment begins two (2) years after completion of National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) program
- For students who do not participate in NYSC, repayment begins two years after graduation
- Grace period allows graduates to secure employment before repayment starts

b) Repayment Amount:
- 10% of monthly income for employed graduates
- Minimum repayment amount applies
- Repayment continues until full loan amount is recovered

c) Repayment Methods:
- Direct debit from salary account (for employed graduates)
- Bank transfer through NELFUND portal
- Mobile banking options
- Automatic deduction from bank account

d) Early Repayment:
- Borrowers can make early repayments without penalty
- Early repayment reduces total interest burden (though loans are interest-free, administrative fees may apply)

7. COVERED INSTITUTIONS
-----------------------

NELFUND loans are available to students in the following types of public institutions:

a) Federal Universities:
- All federal universities across Nigeria
- Must be accredited by the National Universities Commission (NUC)

b) State Universities:
- All state-owned universities
- Must be accredited by NUC

c) Federal Polytechnics:
- All federal polytechnics
- Accredited by the National Board for Technical Education (NBTE)

d) State Polytechnics:
- All state-owned polytechnics
- Accredited by NBTE

e) Colleges of Education:
- Federal and state colleges of education
- Accredited by the National Commission for Colleges of Education (NCCE)

f) Technical Colleges:
- Public technical and vocational education institutions
- Accredited by relevant regulatory bodies

Note: Private institutions are NOT covered under this scheme.

8. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)
-------------------------------------

Q1: Is the loan interest-free?
A: Yes, NELFUND loans are completely interest-free. However, administrative fees may apply.

Q2: Can I apply if I'm already in my final year?
A: Yes, as long as you meet all eligibility criteria and have remaining academic sessions.

Q3: What happens if I drop out or change institutions?
A: You must notify NELFUND immediately. Repayment terms may be adjusted based on circumstances.

Q4: Can I apply for multiple academic sessions?
A: Yes, you can apply for each academic session, subject to maintaining eligibility.

Q5: What if my guarantor cannot fulfill their obligation?
A: You must provide a new guarantor or alternative security as approved by NELFUND.

Q6: How long does the application process take?
A: Typically 2-4 weeks from application submission to disbursement, depending on document verification.

Q7: Can I defer repayment if I'm unemployed?
A: Yes, you can apply for deferment with proper documentation of unemployment status.

Q8: What happens if I default on repayment?
A: Defaulters may face legal action, blacklisting from future loans, and other penalties as specified in the Act.

Q9: Is there a maximum loan amount?
A: The loan covers full tuition fees and up to N50,000 maintenance allowance per session.

Q10: Can international students apply?
A: No, only Nigerian citizens are eligible for NELFUND loans.

9. APPLICATION DEADLINES
------------------------

- Applications are accepted throughout the academic year
- Early application is recommended to ensure timely processing
- Applications for each academic session should be submitted at least 4 weeks before the session begins
- Late applications may be considered on a case-by-case basis

11. IMPORTANT NOTES
-------------------

- All information provided must be accurate and verifiable
- False information may lead to disqualification and legal consequences
- Keep copies of all submitted documents
- Regularly check your email and NELFUND portal for updates
- Maintain good academic standing to remain eligible
- Notify NELFUND of any changes in your circumstances (institution transfer, course change, etc.)

12. REPAYMENT CALCULATION EXAMPLES
-----------------------------------

Example 1: Graduate earning N100,000/month
- Repayment: 10% of N100,000 = N10,000/month
- If total loan is N500,000, repayment period = 50 months (approximately 4 years)

Example 2: Graduate earning N200,000/month
- Repayment: 10% of N200,000 = N20,000/month
- If total loan is N500,000, repayment period = 25 months (approximately 2 years)

13. GUARANTOR REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------

Guarantors must:
- Be Nigerian citizens
- Have valid NIN and BVN
- Be employed or have verifiable source of income
- Not be defaulters on any loan
- Be willing to assume responsibility if borrower defaults
- Provide employment letter or business registration documents
- Provide recent bank statements (minimum 6 months)

14. APPEAL PROCESS
-------------------

If your application is rejected, you can:
1. Review the rejection reason on your portal
2. Submit additional documents if required
3. File an appeal through the NELFUND portal
4. Appeals are reviewed within 10-15 working days
5. Final decision is communicated via email

15. LOAN RENEWAL
----------------

For continuing students:
- Must reapply for each academic session
- Must maintain satisfactory academic progress
- Must not have defaulted on previous loan installments
- Renewal process is typically faster than initial application
